Postdoctoral Researcher in Astrophysics

My scientific goal is to understand the physics of the accretion discs around compact objects. Various models are used to describe those objects depending on what processes we focus on. The interesting point about accretion discs is that they appear at various scales of the Universe, around planets, stars in formation or at evolved stages, around neutrons stars and black holes, and at bigger stage in active galactic nuclei.

Facts

Entering in the details, at the beginning of my research career, one of my primary interest was about the structure of thin massive discs. This research was mainly theoretical and centered on the self-gravitational potential of such discs, meaning the gravitational force produced by the disc itself. The structure and stability of these discs are generally due to the effect of a central source; however, in some systems the self-gravity force produced by the disc itself can be significant and important in the dynamical balance. This is the case for sufficiently massive and/or extended discs, present, for instance, at some stage of the stellar formation. My following interest went to thick accretion discs which can be relevant to understand the central power sources in Active Galactic Nuclei or at the early stage of stars formation. They can also be involved in the generation of bipolar outflows or jets.

Thus, nowadays I am working on equilibrium configurations of accretions discs modeled by a charged perfect (self-gravitating) fluids in rotation embedded in a magnetic field. The described first studies have been done in the Newtonian regime and the more recent ones in the General Relativity framework. This works led to several papers in scientific journals and presentations (talks and posters) at national and international conferences. Since the beginning, I also dedicated my working time to teach at university level. During my PhD, I gave tutorials at BSc level of « Fluid Mechanics ». Moreover, during the first years of my current post-doc position at the Bremen University, I have been in charge of tutorials of « Introduction to General Relativity » at MSc level. Finally last year, I led a lecture of « Astronomy and Accretion Discs » for MSc students.
